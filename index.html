Projects


I. Pynn Development

April 2018 - Ongoing


Pynn is an experience based, multimedia platform that will change the way we use social media. Pynn, in it's first iteration Synthesis Phase of the Inerative Design Process , takes a different approach towards the way we share content by moving the focus from a user's profile to the user's experiences. Users share media consisting of video, photos and text with their connections (friends/followers). Pynn compiles this media into a sharible package, allowing other users the ability to share those same experiences.

Pynn is currently being written in Android Studio, leveraging the scalable flexibility of the IntelliJ Java framwork. View Wireframes / View IDP documentation.


June 2018 - Ongoing

II. rubiKGo


rubikGo is an algorithm-based program that is currently being designed to solve any possible combination of a rubik cube. The algorithm will leverage the lighting-fast, low-level computing power of C++ to quickly provide solutions. rubikGo is currently in the program architecture phase of design and will soon be moving to the program development phase. The prospective plan for rubikGo is to merge the backend complex algorithm with a GUI to create a workable model that could be leveraged by users to test themselves agains an algorithm that will always find the fastest solution.

RubikGo is currently in the system architecture phase and will be moving to the development phase. The program will be written in C++.


III. Gangl

September 2017 - December 2017


Gangl is an application designed by a Systems Design Team to improve the mental health of University students by managing their workload through a simple UI and reminder system. Gangl was developed using an iterative design process beginning with a problem statement, that developed into a low fidelity prototype, medium fidelity prototype and finally a functional high fidelity prototype.

Gangl incorperated user testing, and the usage of Marvel, and Invision app for the purposes of rapid prototyping and extensive design iteration. It was developed by the Gangl Design Team using Android Studio.

Check out the design process video or view design drocess documentation.

IV. Waterloo Rocketry Team


Waterloo Rocketry is an Undergraduate design team at the University of Waterloo. Yup... we put the phrase "It's not rocket science" to shame. As an active member of the Payload sub-team, I designed and developed one of the deadweight cubsats for the Vidar III Rocket. I additionally designed the eye-bolt that secures the rocket's recovery parachute to the rocket's fuselage.

The design of these rocket components and their respective sub components was done using SolidWorks CAD software. SolidWorks provided the ability to calculate critical component features such as mass and center of gravity to the high level of accuracy required for a rocket. The Design projects also required innovative design solutions to accomedate financial, material and manufacturing constraints.

I. Systems Design Engineering, The University of Waterloo

September 2017 - May 2023


Wait... What is Systems Design?

(in short) Systems design covers technical, environmental, socioeconomic and political aspects of the engineering process using systems design methodology.

Systems Design Engineering transends the traditional fields of engineering and works to combine the best of all worlds. Systems promotes innovative optimized solutions to some of todays most challenging problems by tackling them from a socioeconomic perspective. Systems Engineers look for the problem first, and then derive a solution, rather than attempt to apply a solution to a problem.

Learn more about Systems Design Engineering or Read about System's Leading research in the field of engineering.
