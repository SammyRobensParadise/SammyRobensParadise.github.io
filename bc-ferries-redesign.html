BC Ferries Redesign 2019

A case study looking at improving the digital experience of BC ferries

Why The Case Study?

BC ferries is the largest ferry service is canada and serves more than 6 million people annually. it is the life line for thousands of British Columbians across the province.

Although BC Ferries plays a critical role in maintaining the lives of thousands of people, the service has failed to maintain and update their digital presence and provide a clear and positive digital user experience. As a British Columbian, and an engineer studying Systems Design, at the University of Waterloo, the potential for growth and improvement provided an inviting oppertunity for a re-design, examining the "pain points" for users during the digial experience

User Interviews: the Problem Space

Before I even start designing and first conduct user interviews and walkthroughs.

What type of user interviews? I spend a total of 6 hours on ferries conducting user interviews. I asked patrons of all ages and backgrounds about their experience using the BC Ferries website and where there frustrations were. Talking to stakeholders builds empathy with users and helps identify the key problems for users.

Mockups and Prototypes

Once the problem space is defined, we can begin designing and prototyping solutions

To prototype designs and wireframes, I use Adobe Experience Designer, Illustrator and Indesign to creat mockups and prototypes that I can present to users for feedback. using theses tools, designs can be quickly adjusted and iterated for improvements before development even starts.
